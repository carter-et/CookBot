<p>Sometimes the best recipes happen by accident. This is certainly one of them!</p>
<p>I had leftover melted chocolate from another recipe, so I did what any logical person would do: I smeared some almond butter on a date, dunked it, and rolled it in shredded coconut. The rest is history!</p>
<p>Let us show you how easy this recipe is!</p>
<p>Made with just <strong>5-ingredients</strong> and ready in just <strong>15 minutes</strong>, these satisfying, delicious treats are a must-try.</p>

<span id="more-42138"></span>
<p>First, the chocolate and coconut oil are melted in the microwave or on the stovetop. The result is a smooth and creamy mixture perfect for dipping.</p>
<p>The dates are then pitted, creating a perfect slot for smearing a teaspoon of almond butter. Swoon!</p>
<p>Press the sides of the date together so the almond butter doesn’t fall out, and these little gems are ready for dipping!</p>

<p>The almond-butter-filled dates are then dipped in the melted chocolate mixture and rolled in shredded coconut. The result: <strong>Chocolate Snowballs</strong>. Festive much?</p>
<p>You can let the chocolate set if you’d like. But if you’re anything like us, these delicious snowballs won’t make it that far!</p>

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Add dark chocolate and coconut oil to a small mixing bowl and melt in 30 second increments in the microwave (or over a double boiler on the stovetop). Stir until creamy and smooth. Set aside. Set a fork nearby for dipping dates.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Pour shredded coconut in a small mixing bowl and set aside. Set a fork nearby for tossing the dates.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Carefully remove pits from dates, leaving a hollow center in the middle where you’ll place the almond butter. Arrange on a parchment-lined baking sheet for easy cleanup.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Smear 1/2 tsp almond butter in the center of each date, being careful not to overload it, or it will run out the sides when you’re rolling them — not the worst, just a little messy.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Next, smash the ends in toward each other and use the palms of your hands to roll the date into a ball shape. Some of the almond butter may squish out, but that’s okay! It’s getting dunked in chocolate soon anyway.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Place dates one at a time in the chocolate bowl and flip around until coated. Then lift up with a fork and tap off excess.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Add directly to the shredded coconut and use the fork to toss it around until coated. Shake off excess and return to baking sheet. Repeat until all dates are coated.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Enjoy immediately — yay! — or wait until chocolate is fully set. Store in a sealed container for 3-4 days. They will last in the refrigerator up to 1 week, or in the freezer up to 1 month! Let thaw/come to room temperature before enjoying, or the date can be a little tough.</p></div>
</li>
</ol>
