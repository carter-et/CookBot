As the name suggests, Mizutaki is a Japanese hot pot dish where the ingredients, mainly chicken, are cooked in water or simple kombu dashi without any seasoning.</p> <p>Like other hot pot dishes, Mizutaki is typically cooked communally at the table with a portable butane burner. doesn’t drop too fast.</p> <h3>Step 3</h3> <p><img alt="How to Heat Sake at Home | Easy Japanese Recipes at JustOneCookbook.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-93128 nitro-lazy nitro-og-width="800" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" width="800"/></p> <p>Insert the tokkuri in the container and measure the temperature. It’s important to use boiled water and not warm water because the alcohol content will escape if heated for too long.</p> <h3>Step 4</h3> <p><img alt="How to Heat Sake at Home | Easy Japanese Recipes at JustOneCookbook.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-93129 nitro-lazy nitro-lazy-empty" data-pin-description="How to Heat Sake at Home | Easy Japanese Recipes at JustOneCookbook.com" data-pin-title="Mizutaki (Chicken Hot Pot) 水炊き" height="533" id="MTU4MjM4NzEzOS4wMDQ2" nitro-lazy-src="https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/How-to-Warm-Sake-0930.jpg" nitro-lazy-srcset="https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/How-to-Warm-Sake-0930.jpg 800w, https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/How-to-Warm-Sake-0930-300x200.jpg 300w" nitro-og-height="533" nitro-og-width="800" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" width="800"/></p> <p>After 2-3 min, sake should be around 104 F. Warm sake can be enjoyed anywhere from 105 ºF (40 ºC) to 130 ºF (55 ºC) and it’ll actually taste different at 110, 120, and 130 degrees F. Taste test and see what temperature is your personal preference.</p> <p><strong>Note:</strong> The boiling point of alcohol is much lower than water at 173 F (78 C) so heating sake above 131 F (55 C) is not recommended.</p> <h2>Ingredient Substitutions and Suggestions</h2> <ul> <li><strong>Kombu (Dried Kelp) – </strong>Kombu is the most IMPORTANT ingredient that defines the authentic and sophisticated Japanese flavor. If you’re new to Japanese cooking, I recommend <a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/kombu/">learning more about kombu</a> and use it for this hot pot. Soup broth made with kombu kelp is subtle yet filled with umami. It has the taste that you can’t quite describe, and not ordinary chicken soup.</li> <li><strong>Chicken –</strong> Chicken thighs have more flavor which helps to make really good soup stock for the hot pot. It’s much preferred than chicken breasts, especially if we’re not cooking chicken bones for hours.</li> <li><strong>Vegetables –</strong> Use what you can get but keep it simple. Use ingredients that do not overpower the delicious chicken soup.  Cabbage, leeks, bok choy, etc are great.</li> <li><strong>Mushrooms –</strong> Feel free to use any kind of mushrooms including enoki mushrooms, button mushrooms, and cremini mushrooms.</li> <li><strong>Tofu –</strong> You can skip or use Aburaage (deep fry tofu pouch).</li> </ul> <p><img alt="Dipping sauce and condiments to go with, including green onion, yuzu kosho, shichimi togarashi." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-93118 nitro-lazy nitro-lazy-empty" data-pin-description="Mizutaki or Japanese Chicken Hot Pot is a dish that chicken, assorted vegetables, mushrooms, and tofu are cooked in a light yet delicate kombu dashi broth. You can add garnish and condiments to the ponzu like chopped scallions, yuzu kosho, and Shichimi Togarashi (Japanese 7 spice).</p> <p>If you can’t find ponzu in your local Japanese or Asian grocery store, you can <a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/ponzu-sauce/">make this ponzu sauce at home</a>. I make and keep this versatile sauce all the time in my refrigerator. Even though I can get a bottle of ponzu at a Japanese grocery store, homemade ponzu sauce is really easy to make and delicious.</p> <p>Unlike other hot pot that contains seasonings, chicken-based soup broth won’t become salty after simmering for a long time, and it only tastes better!
