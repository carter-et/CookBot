<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’ve followed along for a while, you likely know we have a thing for <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/5-ingredient-vegan-snickers-bars/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Snickers bars</a> (and <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/no-bake-almond-butter-cup-bars/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">candy bars</a> in general).</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Another favorite? PayDays — delicious caramel nougat coated in salty peanuts. What’s not to love?!</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">These <strong>2-ingredient</strong>, <strong>15-minute</strong> vegan PayDays are my healthier, vegan-ized take on the classic candy bar.</span></p>
<p><iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="380" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/6xtGXba_2A4" width="680"></iframe></p>

<p><span id="more-42133"></span>It starts with adding fresh pitted dates to a food processor along with a little sea salt (optional) for enhanced deliciousness.</p>
<p>The dates get processed into small bits, clump together in a ball, and are then ready to form into bites. We’re already off to a good start.</p>
<p><img alt="Rolling a homemade Vegan PayDay Bite inspired by PayDay Candy Bars" class="aligncenter wp-image-47042 size-full" data-jpibfi-post-excerpt="" data-jpibfi-post-title="2-Ingredient Vegan PayDay Bites" data-jpibfi-post-url="https://minimalistbaker.com/2-ingredient-vegan-payday-bites/" data-jpibfi-src="https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/03/2-Ingredient-Vegan-Pay-Day-Bites-1456-v2-06-min.jpg" data-pin-description="EASY 2-Ingredient Vegan PayDay Bites! <p>Next, roll the bites in roasted, salted nuts to give these candies a satisfying crunch!</p>
<p>Any remaining nuts are more finely mixed in the food processor and used to coat the surface of the bites.</p>

<p>We hope you LOVE these candies. They’re:</p>
<p>Sweet<br/>
Salty<br/>
Crunchy<br/>
Naturally-sweetened<br/>
Fiber-rich<br/>
&amp; So delicious!</p>
<p>These bites make the <em>perfect</em> treat to enjoy this Halloween. They’re also a healthier dessert or snack to have around the house any time of year when salty-sweet cravings strike.</p>

<h2 class="wprm-recipe-name">2-Ingredient Vegan PayDay Bites</h2>
<div class="wprm-recipe-summary">
<p>These caramelly, delicious, 2-Ingredient vegan PayDay-inspired bites are a healthier take on the classic candy bar. Gluten-free, naturally-sweetened, and only 15 minutes to make!</p> </div>

<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Ingredients</h3>
<div class="wprm-unit-conversion-container"><a class="wprm-unit-conversion wprmpuc-active" data-recipe="42134" data-system="1" href="#">US Customary</a> - <a class="wprm-unit-conversion" data-recipe="42134" data-system="2" href="#">Metric</a></div> <div class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-group">

<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-unit">cup</span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-name">roasted salted nuts </span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-notes">(we chose peanuts and cashews // or sub dessicated coconut)</span>

<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-notes">(sticky and fresh!* // measured after pitting // 1 cup contains ~19 dates)</span>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-ingredient">
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-amount">1</span>

<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-notes">(<strong><em>optional</em></strong>)</span>
</li>
</ul>

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Add nuts to a <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QsIWfOK_FZzx2mF48yEXYycAAAFpK7ygYAEAAAFKAa8ekAA/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00LBZOYAK/?creativeASIN=B00LBZOYAK&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=zOVO9eTDqF6tSr6olo56SA&amp;slotNum=7&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">food processor</a> and pulse several times to loosely “chop.” You aren’t looking for a meal — just chopped. Set aside on a plate or in a shallow bowl.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>To the <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QsIWfOK_FZzx2mF48yEXYycAAAFpK7ygYAEAAAFKAa8ekAA/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00LBZOYAK/?creativeASIN=B00LBZOYAK&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=zOVO9eTDqF6tSr6olo56SA&amp;slotNum=7&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">food processor</a>, add pitted dates and sea salt and process until small bits remain or a ball forms. Scoop out ~16 even bites (you can use <a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B016QDV7GK/?creativeASIN=B016QDV7GK&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=dzaV1mdlg9HH2q08lHw2YQ&amp;slotNum=30&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">this scoop</a>!) and roll them into balls with your hands. They will be a little sticky — that’s okay!</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Begin rolling date bites into the nut mixture to coat, pressing down gently to encourage sticking. Continue until each date bite is thoroughly coated with nuts. (Optional: To coat even more thoroughly, you can pulse any remaining nuts into a loose meal in the food processor and then roll date bites through again to coat — the finer texture will cause more nuts to stick).</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Enjoy as is or transfer to the refrigerator to chill for 1 hour. Store leftovers covered in the refrigerator up to 2 weeks or the freezer up to 1 month (sometimes longer). If frozen, let thaw slightly before enjoying.</p></div>
</li>
</ol>
</div>

<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Notes</h3>
<p>*It’s important to use dates that are fresh and sticky. If your dates are hard and dried out, try this: Soak in warm water for 10 minutes, then drain thoroughly to bring them back to life. Proceed with the recipe as instructed! <br/>*Nutrition information is a rough estimate calculated without optional salt and with 3/4 cup peanuts and 1/2 cup cashews for 16 bites.</p>
