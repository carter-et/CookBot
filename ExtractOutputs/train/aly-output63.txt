<p>When you want sweet potatoes and you want them <strong><em>fast</em></strong>, this is how to do it!</p>
<p>Not only is this method quick, but it produces perfectly tender sweet potatoes with a slightly caramelized edge — as if you had roasted them in the oven. Swoon!</p>
<p>Plus, this version requires only <strong>1 pan</strong>, <strong>2 ingredients</strong>, and <strong>8 minutes</strong> to prepare (because who has time for roasting some days?). Let’s do this!</p>

<span id="more-49416"></span>
<p>It starts with slicing the sweet potato slightly less than 1/4 inch thick. The more even the slices, the more evenly they will cook.</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img alt="Cooking sweet potato slices in a cast iron skillet" class="wp-image-49351" data-jpibfi-post-excerpt="" data-jpibfi-post-title="8-Minute Sweet Potatoes" data-jpibfi-post-url="https://minimalistbaker.com/8-minute-sweet-potatoes/" data-jpibfi-src="https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/DELICIOUS-8-Minute-Sweet-Potatoes-Cooked-on-the-stovetop-with-a-lid-for-SUPER-quick-tender-potatoes-plantbased-glutenfree-minimalistbaker-sweetpotato-recipe-6.jpg" data-pin-description="DELICIOUS 8 Minute Sweet Potatoes! <p>Then add to a hot greased skillet (we prefer cast iron) and cover with a lid. Covering them allows the potatoes to soften from the steam, which accelerates the cooking process. </p>
<p>That’s the beauty of this method: <strong>They’re steaming and browning at the same time!</strong></p>
<p>Once golden brown on the underside, flip and repeat until they are fork tender and browned on both sides. It’s really that simple.</p>

<h2 class="wprm-recipe-name">8-Minute Sweet Potatoes</h2>
<div class="wprm-recipe-summary">
<p>Our go-to method for quick and easy sweet potatoes! Slicing thin and quickly sautéing while covered yields the perfect, tender sweet potato rounds! Just 2 ingredients, 1 pan, and 8 minutes required!</p> </div>

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Slice sweet potatoes slightly less than 1/4 inch thick and leave the skin on.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Heat a large skillet (we prefer <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QhicFft86lXvb6A_RScuPWAAAAFuk8q9ngEAAAFKAe6p7Qw/http://www.amazon.com/dp/B00IWSQCI6/?creativeASIN=B00IWSQCI6&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=MvoQmCzVrfGgh-IlNu2RGg&amp;slotNum=27&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">cast iron</a>) over medium-high heat. Once hot, reduce heat to medium and add oil to pan. Swirl to coat.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Add sweet potatoes in an even layer, sprinkle with salt (optional), and cover. Cook for about 3-4 minutes or until the underside is golden brown. Then flip and cover again. Reduce heat slightly if cooking too quickly. Cook for about 3 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are golden brown on both sides and fork tender.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Enjoy as is! We love these as part of breakfast bowls, with hummus, or just drizzled with tahini!</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Store leftovers covered in the refrigerator up to 2-3 days. Not freezer friendly. Reheat on the stovetop over medium heat for best results.</div>
</li>
</ol>
</div>
