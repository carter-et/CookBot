<p>If you couldn’t already tell, falafel is one of our <em>absolute</em> <strong>favorite</strong> Mediterranean dishes (see exhibits <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/better-than-restaurant-falafel-vegan-gluten-free/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">A</a>, <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/baked-falafel-burgers/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">B</a>, <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-vegan-falafel/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">C</a>, <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/classic-vegan-falafel-gf/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">D</a>, and <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/baked-quinoa-black-bean-falafel/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">E</a>).</p>
<p>And although these falafel are amazing on their own, adding them to pita wraps with hummus and greens brings them to the next level. Plus, they’re incredibly simple to make — coming together in just <strong>30 minutes</strong>.</p>
<p><img alt="Cutting board with ingredients for making our Kale Falafel Hummus Wraps recipe" class="aligncenter wp-image-43197 size-full" data-jpibfi-post-excerpt="" data-jpibfi-post-title="Kale Falafel Hummus Wraps (30 Minutes!)" data-jpibfi-post-url="https://minimalistbaker.com/kale-falafel-hummus-wraps/" data-jpibfi-src="https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-01.jpg" data-pin-description="DELICIOUS Kale Falafel Hummus Wraps! Quick, flavorful, and so satisfying #vegan #glutenfree #falafel #plantbased #recipe #minimalistbaker" data-pin-title="Kale Falafel Hummus Wraps (30 Minutes!)" height="2182" sizes="(max-width: 960px) 100vw, (max-width: 1149px) 590px, 720px" src="https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-01.jpg" srcset="https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-01.jpg 1456w, https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-01-200x300.jpg 200w, https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-01-768x1151.jpg 768w, https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-01-683x1024.jpg 683w, https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-01-600x899.jpg 600w, https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-01-680x1019.jpg 680w" width="1456"/><span id="more-43196"></span></p>
<p>The falafel are packed with flavor (hello, garlic and cumin!), loaded with <strong>fiber and protein</strong> from chickpeas and oat flour, and have plenty of <strong>green vibes from fresh kale</strong>! The result is a quick-and-easy, delicious falafel that’s secretly good for you.</p>
<p><img alt="Healthy Kale Falafel cooking in a cast-iron skillet" class="aligncenter wp-image-43199 size-full" data-jpibfi-post-excerpt="" data-jpibfi-post-title="Kale Falafel Hummus Wraps (30 Minutes!)" data-jpibfi-post-url="https://minimalistbaker.com/kale-falafel-hummus-wraps/" data-jpibfi-src="https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Kale-Falafel-Hummus-Wraps-1456-v1-03.jpg" data-pin-description="DELICIOUS Kale Falafel Hummus Wraps! <p>This recipe is <strong>made using a food processor</strong>, and once the falafel is formed into patties, it’s ready for cooking!</p>
<p>Unlike traditional falafel, which is deep-fried, <strong>this version is lightly fried in a skillet</strong> until gently browned on both sides. The result is a crispy outside and tender (not dry) center. Serious yum.</p>
<p><iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="380" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/-TTCGB5Dnac" width="680"></iframe></p>
<p>In a matter of minutes, it’s time to assemble your wraps. We like to use pita bread for the wrap, lettuce for greens, and hummus for extra flavor.</p>
<p>But the possibilities are endless…think <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/6-ingredient-vegan-tzatziki/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Vegan Tzatziki</a>, <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/3-ingredient-tahini-sauce-everything/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">3-Ingredient Tahini Sauce</a>, or even <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/spicy-habanero-hot-sauce/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Habanero Hot Sauce</a>!</p>

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Add kale and garlic to a <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QlYJzBfLXHrk7QR0eDxMljgAAAFqR0iFpAEAAAFKAa31TTc/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00LBZOYAK/?creativeASIN=B00LBZOYAK&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=maR8uXO-WvGcOyTJ4wLqLg&amp;slotNum=7&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">food processor</a> and pulse to chop into small bits. Then add chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, cumin, and salt and pulse to combine. Mixture shouldn’t be puréed, just combined.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Once incorporated, transfer mixture to a medium mixing bowl (or stir directly in food processor) and introduce 1/4 cup oat flour (amount as original recipe is written // adjust if altering batch size). Add oat flour another 1 Tbsp (5.6 g) at a time until the mixture is thick enough to handle — adding roughly 2-4 Tbsp.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Taste and adjust seasonings as desired. I added a touch more salt, cumin, and lemon juice. Scoop out heaping 2-Tbsp portions of falafel dough and use your hands to form them into approximately 1/2-inch thick patties. As written, recipe makes ~9 falafel.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p><i><b>NOTE</b>: If not cooking all of the falafel right away, FREEZE uncooked falafel between layers of parchment paper and store in a container in the freezer up to 1 month. Then just thaw and cook as instructed!</i></p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Heat a large skillet over medium to medium-high heat. Once hot, add 2 Tbsp oil (or lesser end of range if adjusting batch size). Swirl to coat pan. Add 4 falafel (or however many will fit comfortably) to the pan at a time.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Cook until browned on the bottom, checking at the 2-minute mark to ensure they’re not over-browning. If they are, slightly reduce heat. Flip once they're deep golden brown — about 3-4 minutes. Cook until the other side is golden brown as well.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>To assemble, lay pita (or chard) flat, top with 2-3 falafel, a healthy spread of hummus, and greens or herbs, and roll up. Secure with a toothpick or wrap with parchment or foil for easy handling. Store leftover falafel covered in the refrigerator for up to 4-5 days and reheat in a 350 degree F (176 C) oven. See freezing instructions in step 4. </p></div>
</li>
</ol>
</div>

<p>Falafel can be tricky to conquer. More than I’d like to admit, I’ve made recipes before that have failed, resulting in a crumbly sad mess. </p>
<p>But this recipe changed EVERYTHING!</p>
<p>The falafel were perfectly cooked throughout and tasted better than my favorite food cart. The addition of kale really elevated the recipe. Bonus, it’s healthier by including more greens.  </p>
<p>I made 10 balls at roughly 50 grams each, fried in 2 tablespoons of olive oil for 3 minutes on each side. Served on pita with fresh diced tomato, spicy hummus, and a super green mix. I’m saving this one!</p>
