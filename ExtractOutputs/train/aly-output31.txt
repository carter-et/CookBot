Here’s how.</p> <p> </p> <p>1 cup cake flour = 1 cup AP flour - 2 Tbsp AP flour + 2 Tbsp cornstarch</p> <p> </p> <p>Be sure to sift the flour to distribute the cornstarch well before using it in your cake batter.</p> <p> </p> <p><strong>Instant Dry Yeast</strong> If you're using active dry yeast, it requires being activated in a little bit of warm water (110F/43C) before being added to the rest of the ingredients. In this recipe, add the active dry yeast in 110F/43C milk, hotter than the temperature specified in the recipe because if the milk is not hot enough, the yeast won't dissolve nicely. More about the difference between instant dry yeast and active dry yeast, read <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/whats-the-difference-between-active-dry-yeast-and-instant-yeast-54252" rel="noopener" target="_blank">here</a>.</p> <p> </p> <p><strong>Whole milk:</strong> I highly recommend using whole milk instead of reduced fat milk, but it's up to you.</p> <p> </p> <p><strong>Red Bean Paste:</strong> I use coarse red bean paste but you can use fine paste.
