<p>Have you ever tried mujadara? If not, you’re totally missing out.</p>
<h2>What is Mujadara?</h2>
<p>Mujadara is a satisfying vegetarian dish with Middle Eastern and Arab influences. It’s traditionally made with rice, lentils, and caramelized onions, making it incredibly affordable and satisfying.</p>
<p>The spices can range in simplicity from just salt, pepper, and cumin to more complex flavors like cinnamon, lemon peel, and cayenne pepper.</p>
<h2>What Makes our Version Unique?</h2>
<ul>
<li>It’s made with cauliflower rice instead of rice to keep it grain-free.</li>
<li>It’s made with way less oil (with options to make it completely oil-free!).</li>
<li>The spices are simplified as we rely on cumin, coriander, and our go-to <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/diy-curry-powder/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">DIY Curry Powder</a>.</li>
<li>The shallots are crispy instead of soft to add more texture!</li>
<li>We include optional dates at the end for a bit of sweet to balance out the salt.</li>
</ul>

<p>To start, cook your lentils in vegetable broth so they get tender and flavorful but not mushy. Then prepare your shallots!</p>
<p><strong>To keep this recipe grain-free</strong> we opted to toss our shallots in chickpea flour and salt and use <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-make-cauliflower-rice/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">cauliflower rice</a> instead of rice.</p>
<p><strong>To keep this recipe low/no oil</strong>, we include options for baking instead of pan-frying so the shallots still get nice and golden brown without the extra oil.</p>
<p><strong>If you’d prefer more tender vs. crispy shallot</strong>, simply pan sauté (opting out of the crispy coating) in a little oil or water over medium-low heat until golden brown and tender.</p>

<p>Sauté with curry powder and cumin seed, the cooked lentils, and half of the crispy shallot, reserving the remaining shallot for serving!</p>
