Simmer turnips in a savory dashi broth</h3> <p>Turnips are gently simmered in a golden dashi broth to absorb all the wonderful, umami-rich flavors.</p> <h3>2. Cook ground chicken in a sweet soy sauce</h3> <p>While simmering turnips, you would cook the ground chicken in a separate pan. This seasoned ground chicken dish is called Soboro in Japanese, and you may see this dish used in <a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/tori-soboro-donburi/">Soboro Donburi</a>.</p> <p><strong>If you’re vegetarian or vegan</strong>, you can skip the chicken or swap it with firm tofu. If you skip, combine the Soboro Seasonings and Slurry together and add into the broth.</p> <h3>3. Put everything together and make a thick savory sauce</h3> <p>When the turnips are tender, you add the Soboro into the dashi broth. Finally, add the slurry to the broth, which makes a delicious, thicker, richer, and more luxurious sauce.</p> <p style="text-align:center;"><img alt="A beautiful Japanese bowl containing simmered Get the same size turnips</h3> <p>The size of the turnips doesn’t matter. If you can find smaller ones than mine, your cooking time will be less. If you get bigger turnips, you will need to be patient and cook a little longer.</p> <p>However, it’s important to choose similar size turnips so they will be done cooking at the same time.</p> <h3>2. Cut off the bottom of the turnips</h3> <p>By cutting off the bottom of the turnips, they will stand up straight while being simmered. Plus, you can prevent the stalks from getting overcooked. When you serve, turnips will look great standing on its own.</p> <h3>3. Simmer turnips for 5 minutes before adding seasonings</h3> <p>Give the turnips a headstart by simmering them first before seasoning.
