Buy “Three Ladies Brand” rice paper</strong></h3> <p>Some of you have asked for my recommendation for rice paper brand. I’ve tried a few and my favorite choice goes to “Three Ladies Brand”.  The rice papers are easy to work with, and I find it easy to detach the spring rolls without tearing the rice papers when they stick to each other.</p> <p><img alt="Rice Paper" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-40447 nitro-lazy nitro-lazy-empty" data-pin-title="Fresh Spring Rolls with Yakiniku 焼肉の生春巻き" height="400" id="MTU4MjM4OTY5NC4wMTE3" nitro-lazy-src="https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/source/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Rice-Paper-600x400.jpg" nitro-lazy-srcset="https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Rice-Paper.jpg 600w, https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/source/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Rice-Paper-300x200.jpg 300w, https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/source/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Rice-Paper-350x233.jpg 350w, https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/source/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Rice-Paper-259x173.jpg 259w, https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/source/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Rice-Paper-150x100.jpg 150w" nitro-og-height="400" nitro-og-width="600" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" width="600"/></p> <h3><strong>2. Start with less filling and “tuck tightly and roll”</strong></h3> <p>If you’ve never worked with rice papers for spring rolls before, it’s a bit like making pancakes: you just need a few practice runs to get better at it. To start off, do not over pile the filling. It’s easier to work with less than more. Once you do a few rolls, you will get better at judging the right amount of filling. When you’re ready to roll the wrappers, make sure your first fold over is tight enough to cover the ingredients. Then fold the side edges towards the middle to secure a nice package. Then give it another tight roll to finish off.</p> <h3><strong>3. Adjust the amount of sugar</strong></h3> <p>For those who are careful with their sugar intake, the amount of sugar (3 Tbsp sugar) that goes into the sauce might raise your eyebrow.  Please feel free to adjust the amount, but remember that you need to counter the salty flavor from soy sauce with a sweetener.  You can swap the sugar with honey or maple syrup if you like, but the final taste will be different from my recipe.</p> <p>If you prefer the original taste of Japanese Yakiniku Sauce, follow this recipe, but don’t go crazy with the sauce.
