<p>Mung beans are <a href="https://www.pingminghealth.com/article/281/mung-beans-can-clear-heat-reduce-acne-and-detox-the-body/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">kind of magnificent</a>. Ever tried them?</p>
<p>If not, you should! They’re <strong>easy to digest</strong> and <strong>quick-cooking</strong>, and they <strong>help rid the body of toxins</strong> and <strong>cleanse the liver</strong>! How cool is that?</p>
<p>Ready your mung beans and let’s make soup!</p>

<span id="more-30611"></span>
<p>This <strong>10-ingredient, oil-free, grain-free soup</strong> starts by soaking and cooking your mung beans. You can soak them overnight, or if you’re in a hurry, you can do it in 1 hour! Once soaked, they cook up in 15-20 minutes. Then your soup is already nearly halfway done.</p>

<p>In a separate pot, you’ll add the basics: onion, carrot, and celery. They’re followed by green beans, kale, zucchini, and a wave of flavor with fire-roasted tomatoes and <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-1-pot-vegetable-broth/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">vegetable broth</a>! We like making our own vegetable broth, but you can easily go for store-bought to save time (our favorite is the brand <a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B000R7V30M/ref=as_at?creativeASIN=B000GIGZXM&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=MAIU1ohwQPZCzdQRF79RNA&amp;slotNum=4&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Imagine</a>).</p>
<p>Once all the ingredients are added, all that’s left to do is let it simmer away to perfection.</p>

<p>We hope you all LOVE this soup! It’s:</p>
<p>Hearty<br/>Healthy<br/>Rich<br/>Satisfying<br/>Loaded with vegetables<br/>Easy to make<br/>&amp; Incredibly nourishing</p>
<p>This is the perfect soup to get you through cold, dark days or when you need a pick-me-up. We reach for this on days when our bodies need a little comfort, and we love making a large batch and freezing it so it’s always on hand. Amplify its nourishing, cleansing effects by topping with raw garlic and fresh parsley.</p>

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Start by soaking mung beans overnight or employing the quick-soak method found in the notes. Then add soaked, drained mung beans to a large saucepan with water (use 3 cups water per 1 cup dry beans) and kombu (optional) and bring to a boil.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until tender but not mushy – about 17-23 minutes. Drain off any excess liquid and set aside.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">In the meantime, heat a separate large pot over medium heat. Once hot, add water and onion. Sauté for 4-5 minutes or until ender and light golden brown, stirring frequently.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Add carrots and celery and sauté for 4-5 minutes more or until slightly tender, stirring frequently. Then add zucchini, green beans, fire-roasted tomatoes, vegetable broth, and salt and pepper. Stir to combine and increase heat. Bring to a low boil.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Once boiling, reduce heat to a simmer and add cooked (drained) mung beans. Simmer for 10-15 minutes more or until all vegetables are tender.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Add greens and cook 5 minutes more, stirring occasionally. Then remove from heat and let rest 5 minutes.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Serve as is or garnish with fresh garlic and parsley for more flavor and cleansing, nourishing benefits. Flatbread or whole-grain bread would make a delicious side as well as the salads linked above.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Store cooled leftovers in the refrigerator up to 5 days or in the freezer up to 1 month (or more). Reheat leftovers on the stovetop until hot.</div>
</li>
</ol>
