<p>We’ve previously shared a recipe for <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/quinoa-gado-gado-bowl-30-minutes/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Gado Gado</a> — a quick, simple, and flavorful Indonesian-inspired dish perfect for weeknight cooking. We love that recipe so much we wanted to share a slightly different, but equally simple and delicious variation.</p>
<p>This version uses sweet potato instead of carrots, swaps the red cabbage for extra green beans, and includes an optional egg. Oh, and we kicked things up a notch with a <em>spicy</em> version of the peanut sauce. Let’s do this!<i></i></p>

<p><span id="more-43387"></span></p>
<p>The base of this <strong>30-minute recipe</strong> is brown rice. It’s quick and easy to make, plus rich in vitamins and minerals such as selenium, magnesium, and vitamin B3.</p>
<p>To make this bowl <em>extra</em> filling and delicious, we also roasted coated sweet potato rounds until perfectly tender and sweet.</p>

<p>For a pop of green and a bit of crunch, we steamed green beans until just tender, then added them to ice water to stop the cooking and keep them fresh and vibrant.</p>
<p>Not only are green beans delicious, they’re also loaded with vitamins and minerals including vitamins K, C, folate, B2, chromium, calcium, and so many more!</p>

<p>Next comes the peanut sauce! Simply whisk peanut butter, tamari, lime juice, maple syrup, and chili garlic sauce in a mixing bowl. Add water to thin and you’ve got a magical, versatile sauce!</p>
<p>The rice and veggies are divided between serving bowls, topped with cilantro and peanuts, and everything is drizzled with spicy peanut sauce.</p>

<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>In a large saucepan, bring water and brown rice to a boil, then add a pinch of salt, swirl to combine, cover, and reduce heat to simmer. Cook for 20-30 minutes until water is absorbed and rice is tender and fully cooked. Drain off any excess liquid at the end if there is any. Set rice aside and cover to keep warm.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>In the meantime, preheat oven to 400 degrees F (204 C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Add sliced sweet potato and drizzle with avocado oil. Bake for 20-25 minutes. When ready, rounds should be tender and slightly golden brown. Set aside.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>While potato rounds and rice are cooking, steam your green beans in a steamer basket or in the microwave for 3-4 minutes. Alternatively, add to boiling hot water for 1 minute, then transfer to ice water to stop the cooking process (a.k.a. “shocking”). Drain and set aside.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Prepare the peanut sauce by adding all ingredients except water to a small mixing bowl and whisking. Add hot water 1 tablespoon (15 ml) at a time until desired consistency is reached — it should be pourable.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Taste and adjust seasonings as needed, adding more chili garlic sauce for heat, maple syrup for sweetness, or lime juice for acidity.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>To assemble, divide brown rice between 3 dishes and top with roasted sweet potato, steamed green beans, sliced red pepper, bean sprouts, cilantro, and a hard-boiled egg (optional). Serve with spicy peanut sauce and salted roasted peanuts (optional). For extra heat, sriracha or extra chili garlic sauce make great additions.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Best when fresh. Store any leftovers separate from dressing in the refrigerator up to 2 days.</p></div>
</li>
</ol>
