nitro-og-width="800" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" width="800"/></p> <h3>What is White Sauce (Béchamel) and How to Make It</h3> <p>White sauce (ホワイトソース), or also known as Béchamel sauce, is a classic all-purpose French sauce made from butter, flour, and milk. It pretty much forms the basis of French dishes, such as casseroles, cream croquettes, and savory soufflés. And it’s pretty easy and simple to make at home.</p> <p><strong>Here are the 3 simple steps.</strong></p> <ol> <li>Warm up the milk in the microwave or in a pot over the stovetop.</li> <li>Melt the butter, add flour, and whisk until flour is cooked.</li> <li>Gradually add warm milk until thickened to the right consistency.</li> </ol> <p><strong>The Basic White Sauce Ratio</strong></p> <p>A roux is usually a mixture of equal weights of butter and flour.  Here in this recipe, I used 3 tablespoons (42 g) of butter and 3 tablespoons (45 g) of all-purpose flour.  Besides butter, ghee, coconut oil or other fat can be used. You can also use gluten-free flour in place of all-purpose flour.</p> <p>The roux then will be mixed with 2 cups milk, and the amount can be adjustable based on how thick you want the sauce to be. In this recipe, I used 1 cup milk and 1 cup liquid from the stew.</p> <p><img alt="A gray bowl containing Japanese Cream Stew (White Stew) with chicken and vegetables in a savory thick white sauce." class="aligncenter wp-image-75750 size-full nitro-lazy nitro-lazy-empty" data-pin-description="Creamy, hearty, and delicious! Protein</h4> <p>Chicken and pork are typically used as the main protein, but feel free to substitute with other choices. I think seafood works beautifully in the stew. For vegetarian, you can skip protein and use just the vegetables. Perhaps cauliflower or a variety of wild mushrooms as your main ingredient.</p> <h4>2. Vegetables</h4> <p>Onions, potatoes, and carrots are the basic vegetables in the cream stew. Most cream stew has some type of green vegetables; broccoli is a popular choice, then green peas and green beans.</p> <p>For potatoes, Yukon Gold potatoes have a smooth, slightly waxy, and firm texture. Russet potatoes have a creamy, crumbly, starchy texture, but they don’t hold their shape well. If you like to use this type, remember to cut into bigger chunks like I did.</p> <h4>3. Mushrooms</h4> <p>Button mushrooms are commonly used but some people use Japanese mushrooms like shimeji mushrooms and maitake mushrooms. I don’t recommend shiitake mushrooms as they have distinct flavors and fragrance that don’t go well with this dish.</p> <h4>4. Stock</h4> <p>The packaged cream stew roux requires simple water, but when you make a homemade white sauce (bechamel sauce), I recommend using chicken or vegetable broth to create more depth and complexity in the flavors.</p> <h4>5. Other Commonly Used Ingredients</h4> <p>Heavy cream, corn, and cheese are common additions to the cream stew.</p> <p><img alt="A gray bowl containing Japanese Cream Stew (White Stew) with chicken and vegetables in a savory thick white sauce." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-75745 nitro-lazy nitro-lazy-empty" data-pin-description="Creamy, hearty, and delicious! Cut vegetables into uniform sizes or adjust the cutting size.</h4> <p>Each vegetable, whether hard or tender, must be cooked under the same amount of time in the Instant Pot. To achieve the perfect texture, you need to cut the vegetables into uniform sizes so they cook roughly at the same rate.</p> <p>One trick to shorten the cooking time is to cut the tough vegetables slightly smaller sizes so they cook faster.</p> <p>If one vegetable tends to cook too fast, you may need to cut it into slightly bigger size. In this recipe, that’s Russet potatoes.  If you cut the potatoes into the same size as other vegetables, these starchy potatoes will be disintegrated into the stew.</p> <h4>2. Make the white sauce outside of the Instant Pot and add it after pressure cooking</h4> <p>Milk isn’t so great in the Instant Pot as the high heat and pressure separate the milk proteins and leads to curdling. Although it is perfectly safe to eat, it doesn’t look appetizing.</p> <p>Therefore, you have to make the white sauce in a separate pot, and then add the sauce after you’re finished pressure cooking.  All you need to do is to combine and warm everything up on lower heat until the right consistency.</p> <h4>3. Add separately-blanched green vegetables at the end.</h4> <p>Do not cook the green vegetables in the Instant Pot as they will change to dark and sad looking greens.  Instead, blanch the green vegetables of your choice in a separate pot, and add them to reheat together.  This way, you retain the beautiful color in your dish.</p> <p style="text-align:center;"><img alt="A gray bowl containing Japanese Cream Stew (White Stew) with chicken and vegetables in a savory thick white sauce." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-75748 nitro-lazy nitro-lazy-empty" data-pin-description="Creamy, hearty, and delicious!
