<p>This recipe is inspired by a cooking class I recently took in Cabo San Lucas, Mexico, where I learned to make a number of dishes including this <em>gorgeous</em> herbed baked fish with rainbow bell peppers.</p>
<p>The recipe preparation is simple and<strong> requires just 10 ingredients</strong>. Let me show you how easy it is to make this light, flavorful dish!</p>

<p>To start you’ll cook your onions, peppers, and tomatoes down until they’re soft and just tender. They’ll be added over top the fish later and baked, which creates a vibrant top layer and also creates a sauce for the fish.</p>

<p>Next, you’ll season your fish with oil, salt, a little lime juice, and a blend of herbs. Use whatever you hand on hand — we went with basil, cilantro, rosemary, and thyme.</p>

<p>After a quick sear on the stovetop, you’ll transfer to a baking dish, then top with the cooked peppers. After a short bake in the oven, dinner’s ready!</p>

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190C) and set out a 9x13 (or similarly sized) baking dish.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Add fish to a shallow dish or plate, drizzle with oil, and sprinkle with salt, pepper, herbs, and lime juice. Toss to coat and set in the fridge to marinate.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Heat a large pot or <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QriBA2TEiFasPpQyvMHwCN4AAAFq-nqWwgEAAAFKAcebKxM/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01BFFU28I/?creativeASIN=B01BFFU28I&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=gH77fx.1VCuaaHNJRYQ7hw&amp;slotNum=37&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">dutch oven</a> over medium-high heat. Once hot, add oil, onion, tomatoes, garlic, and bell peppers. Season with salt and pepper and stir to coat.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Sauté, stirring frequently, for 8-10 minutes, occasionally covering to allow the peppers to soften. Stop when the peppers are tender but not yet mushy. Set aside.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Heat a large skillet (<a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QnNbSSrsvQ8bMe8X7LUhHZEAAAFq-nmKggEAAAFKAVF3-HI/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00IWSQCI6/?creativeASIN=B00IWSQCI6&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=QCKsTuFZxjnDtRL.6Q55Nw&amp;slotNum=25&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">cast iron</a> is best) over medium-high heat. Once hot, add fish. Cook for 2 minutes, then carefully flip and cook for 2 minutes more. You aren’t looking for the fish to be completely done — just seared on the outside.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Transfer fish to your 9x13 (or similarly sized) baking dish. Top with sautéed peppers, tomatoes, and onion and spread the veggies to achieve an even layer.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Cover with foil and bake for 10 minutes, then remove foil and bake for 3-5 minutes more to allow the top to get slightly browned.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Remove from oven and enjoy. Delicious on its own, or served with <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-make-green-rice/" target="_blank">Green Rice</a>, <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/green-cauliflower-rice/" target="_blank">Green Cauliflower Rice</a>, <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-make-cauliflower-rice/" target="_blank">cauliflower rice</a>, <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-cook-brown-rice-2-ways/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">brown rice</a>, or <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/?s=white+rice" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">white rice</a>.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Store leftovers in the refrigerator up to 2-3 days. Or add to freezer-safe tupperware and freeze up to 1 month (though best when fresh). Reheat in the oven or microwave until warm.</p></div>
</li>
</ol>
