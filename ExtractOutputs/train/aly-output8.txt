The one on the left is spicy, <span style="color:#f00;">Chili</span> Bean Sauce/Paste (<span style="color:#f00;">La</span> Dobanjiang or La Tobanjiang <span style="color:#f00;">辛</span>豆瓣酱), and the one on the right is non-spicy, Fermented Broad Bean Paste (Dobanjiang or Tobanjiang, 豆瓣酱).</p> <p>The Chinese name for this sauce is always the same, 豆瓣酱 for non-spicy and <span style="color:#f00;">辣</span>豆瓣酱 for spicy. However, English names might have “Sauce” on one jar and “Paste” on the other, or “Bean Paste” on one jar and “Broad Bean Paste” on the other.</p> <p>If there’s any confusion, check the ingredients on the label. The first two ingredients should be “fermented broad bean paste” and “soybean”.  If you want to make NON-SPICY ramen, make sure to pick the jar that doesn’t contain chili (You can tell from the color of the paste). Pay extra attention that it is NOT Black Bean Sauce even though the names might seem similar.</p> <h4>Buying Online</h4> <p>I know many of you can’t find this non-spicy bean paste, Dobanjiang or Tobanjiang (shown on the left) at Asian grocery stores.
