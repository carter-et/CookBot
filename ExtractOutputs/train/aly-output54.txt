<p>If given the choice of one final meal, there’s a good chance we’d choose lasagna. No <em>really</em>, we <strong>seriously</strong> love lasagna (exhibits <a aria-label="A (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-vegan-lasagna/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">A</a>, <a aria-label="B (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/sweet-potato-lasagna/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">B</a>, <a aria-label="C (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/lentil-eggplant-lasagna/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">C</a>, <a aria-label="D (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/eggplant-lasagna-roll-ups/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">D</a>, <a aria-label="E (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/8-ingredient-zucchini-lasagna-gf/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">E</a>, and <a aria-label="F (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/spaghetti-squash-lasagna-bake/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">F</a>)! </p>
<p>Lasagna is just one of those meals that’s the perfect balance of comforting, filling, and delicious, <em>and</em> it can feed a lot of people with minimal effort. </p>
<p>This version is our <strong>dairy-free</strong> and <strong>gluten-free</strong> take on classic-style lasagna (we have a <a aria-label="vegan version (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-vegan-lasagna/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">vegan version</a> if that’s more your thing). And it’s about as simple as it gets, requiring just <strong>10 ingredients</strong> to prepare. Let’s do this!</p>

<h2>How to Make Lasagna</h2>
<p>Making lasagna is pretty straightforward in terms of assembly. But the ingredients can be swapped out for just about any dietary preference. Start by preparing your three layers:</p>
<ol><li><strong>Sauce</strong> (ground beef or <a aria-label="lentils (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-vegan-lasagna/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">lentils</a> + marinara + seasonings)</li><li><strong>Noodles</strong> (we chose Capello’s gluten-free &amp; grain-free for this version)</li><li><strong>Cheese</strong> (<a aria-label="homemade dairy-free macadamia nut (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/5-minute-macadamia-cheese-vegan-crudite/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">homemade dairy-free macadamia nut</a> or storebought)</li></ol>
<p>Our sauce is prepared by sautéing garlic, red pepper flake (<em>optional</em>), ground beef, and salt until the meat is browned. Then add in <a aria-label="your favorite marinara sauce (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-1-pot-marinara-sauce/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">your favorite marinara sauce</a> and season further with optional Italian herbs and sweetener.</p>
<p>Next, the noodles are boiled and drained. Or — our preference — opt for no-boil gluten-free lasagna noodles such as Capello’s.</p>
<p>The homemade “cheese” comes next. Simply add macadamia nuts (or <a aria-label="slivered almonds (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/whipped-almond-ricotta-5-ingredients/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">slivered almonds</a> or soaked cashews) to a <a aria-label="food processor (opens in a new tab)" href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QmvCjGwsgNP4Igt5bAF4R-AAAAFvH8T-VgEAAAFKAYDYX6E/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00LBZOYAK/?creativeASIN=B00LBZOYAK&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=y.i9TdK8WokswJ-8.DawJQ&amp;slotNum=7&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow" target="_blank">food processor</a> along with lemon juice, nutritional yeast, garlic powder, and sea salt. Blend it up, slowly adding water until it resembles ricotta cheese! Or, sub store-bought (our favorite being Kite Hill Ricotta).</p>
<p>Finally, let’s layer!</p>

<h2>How to Layer Lasagna</h2>
<p>We like to use a little jingle to remember the order: <strong>Sauce, Noodles, Cheese. (Repeat x2)</strong>. </p>
<p>And then on the fourth repetition: <strong>Sauce, Noodles, SAUCE!</strong></p>
<p>Once you make lasagna enough times, you won’t even have to look up the order!</p>
<p>Then at the very end, add a sprinkle of homemade <a aria-label="vegan parmesan cheese (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-make-vegan-parmesan-cheese/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank"><em>vegan parmesan cheese</em></a> for even more flavor.</p>

<h2>How Long to Bake Lasagna</h2>
<p>To allow the flavors to develop, we like to bake the lasagna until it’s nice and bubbly with crisp edges. </p>
<p>Baking<strong> uncovered</strong> on the <strong>middle rack</strong> at <strong>350 degrees F (176 C)</strong> for <strong>40-45 minutes</strong> is perfect.</p>

<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-name">water</span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-notes">(if using soaked cashews, add 1 Tbsp / 15 ml water at a time)</span>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-group">
<h4 class="wprm-recipe-group-name wprm-recipe-ingredient-group-name">NOODLES</h4>
<ul class="wprm-recipe-ingredients">
<li class="wprm-recipe-ingredient">
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-amount">1</span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-unit">9-oz</span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-name">box gluten-free lasagna noodles </span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-notes">(we like Capello’s brand)</span>
</li>
</ul>

<ul class="wprm-recipe-ingredients">
<li class="wprm-recipe-ingredient">
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-name"><a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-make-vegan-parmesan-cheese/" target="_blank">Vegan Parmesan Cheese</a></span>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-ingredient">
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-name">Fresh herbs </span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-notes">(such as basil or parsley)</span>
</li>

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (176 C). Set out a large baking dish (9×13 inch or similar size) and lightly grease.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Begin by preparing sauce. Heat a large pot or <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QlSlmZRTQbnhJLwuoQxw1e4AAAFvH6E-ggEAAAFKAfsiMqg/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01BFFU28I/?creativeASIN=B01BFFU28I&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=fPQRi-jzmgdqcQlGJqE76w&amp;slotNum=41&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Dutch oven</a> over medium heat. Once hot, add oil, garlic, red pepper flake (optional), beef, and salt and begin sautéing, stirring occasionally for about 8 minutes, or until meat is browned. Use spoon to break meat into small pieces.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>In the meantime, if using noodles that require boiling, bring a large pot of water to a boil (if using no-boil noodles, skip this step). Once boiling, add a healthy pinch of salt and your lasagna noodles (or other pasta shape). Cook according to package instructions to achieve an al dente texture — tender with a little bite. Then drain and set aside. <em>Optional</em>: Drizzle pasta with a little oil to prevent sticking.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>To the pot, add your marinara sauce and stir to combine. Cook on low for about 10 minutes to marry the flavors. Then taste and adjust flavor as needed, adding salt to taste or red pepper flake for heat. You could also add dried Italian spices and/or sweetener of choice to balance the flavors at this time (<em>optional</em>). Turn off heat and set aside.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Next, make your "cheese." Add macadamia nuts (or sub soaked cashews) to a <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QoNN73bUF8Ko5cobU3QaKMwAAAFvH6KBJwEAAAFKAaqLmsE/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00LBZOYAK/?creativeASIN=B00LBZOYAK&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=wv7CAnQRFU8NnlkRavSmDQ&amp;slotNum=7&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">food processor</a> or a <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QrXL9OzdNZbC1R-PlCgcTXEAAAFvJEJy1AEAAAFKAXsw-Ws/https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B008H4SLV6/?creativeASIN=B008H4SLV6&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=663jPIfJ83hFY3P1cWkoKQ&amp;slotNum=13" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">high-speed blender</a> along with lemon juice, nutritional yeast, garlic powder, and sea salt and mix/blend into a loose paste.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Add lesser amount of water (2/3 cup or 120 ml as original recipe is written // adjust if altering batch size). Continue mixing, scraping down sides as needed. Then add more water — 1 Tbsp (15 ml) at a time — until a thick paste forms. I find I get the best texture results with a food processor, but a blender can work, too. It just generally requires more scraping and more liquid. For texture, you’re looking for a spreadable soft “cheese.”</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Taste and adjust flavor as needed, adding more nutritional yeast for cheesy flavor, salt for saltiness, lemon juice for acidity/brightness, or garlic powder for garlic flavor. Blend again to combine.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>To begin assembly, add a thin layer of marinara sauce to your baking dish. Spread to ensure even distribution. Then add a layer of the cooked pasta noodles (or uncooked noodles if using no-boil). Top with a thin layer (roughly 1/3 of the mixture) of your nut cheese and spread into an even layer.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Add another thin layer of marinara sauce and use a spoon to spread over the cheese layer to coat. Then add another layer of noodles. Top with another 1/3 of the cheese and spread into an even layer.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Add another layer of marinara sauce and spread into an even layer. Then top with another layer of noodles and remaining 1/3 of the cheese, and spread into an even layer. Top with another layer of marinara sauce and spread into an even layer.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Top with another layer of noodles and, finally, remaining sauce. Optional: Top with a sprinkle of vegan parmesan cheese.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Cook on the middle rack of your preheated oven for 40-45 minutes uncovered, or until the sauce is bubbly and/or the edges are browned.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Carefully remove from the oven and let cool 5-10 minutes before serving. Then slice, serve, and enjoy. We recommend garnishing with additional vegan parmesan cheese and fresh chopped basil or parsley. See notes for freezing instructions.*</p></div>
</li>
</ol>
</div>

<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Notes</h3>
<p>*To make this recipe freezer friendly, prepare sauce, cheese, and noodles and layer as instructed. Once assembled, cover well and freeze up to 1 month. To bake, preheat oven to 350 degrees F (176 C), cover with foil, and bake for 30 minutes (if thawed) or up to 45 minutes – 1 hour (if frozen). Then remove foil and bake for another 5-10 minutes or until sauce is bubbly and hot.<br>*Nutrition information is a rough estimate calculated with Capello’s lasagna noodles and without optional ingredients.</br></p>
