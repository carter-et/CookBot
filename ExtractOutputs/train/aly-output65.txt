<p>In our quest to uncover the <a aria-label="best ways to make kale taste good (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-make-kale-taste-good-(guide-+-recipes)/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">best ways to make kale taste good</a>, we discovered our <em>favorite</em>, simple way to enjoy cooked kale.</p>
<p>Let us show you how quick and easy this <strong>1-pan sautéed kale</strong> is, and which seasonings make its flavor pop. </p>

<p>It all starts with fresh kale (organic when possible). As for type,<strong> we prefer curly kale</strong> as it’s a bit sturdier and holds up to cooking better than dinosaur/Tuscan kale.</p>
<p>Simply remove the stalky parts of the stem, chop, and set aside.</p>

<p>Heat a pan over medium heat and add oil, kale, and seasonings of choice.</p>
<p>Our go-to seasonings are a pinch of salt and <a aria-label="DIY curry powder (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/diy-curry-powder/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">DIY curry powder</a>. Store-bought curry powder or other spices of choice would work well, too (especially turmeric, cumin, and smoked paprika).</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img alt="Covered pan of kale with curry powder for our simple Sautéed Kale recipe" class="wp-image-48823" data-jpibfi-post-excerpt="" data-jpibfi-post-title="Sautéed Kale with Lemon, Tahini &amp; Hemp Seeds" data-jpibfi-post-url="https://minimalistbaker.com/sauteed-kale-with-lemon-tahini-hemp-seeds/" data-jpibfi-src="https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/QUICK-Sauteed-Kale-with-Lemon-Hemp-Seeds-and-Tahini-10-minutes-6-ingredients-BIG-flavor-plantbased-glutenfree-kale-minimalistbaker-recipe-5.jpg" data-pin-description="QUICK Sauteed Kale with Lemon, Hemp Seeds, and Tahini! <p>Sauté then cover briefly to steam and wilt the kale. You’ll know it’s done when it’s slightly wilted, bright green in color, and golden brown in spots.</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img alt="Sautéing kale in a cast iron skillet" class="wp-image-48824" data-jpibfi-post-excerpt="" data-jpibfi-post-title="Sautéed Kale with Lemon, Tahini &amp; Hemp Seeds" data-jpibfi-post-url="https://minimalistbaker.com/sauteed-kale-with-lemon-tahini-hemp-seeds/" data-jpibfi-src="https://minimalistbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/QUICK-Sauteed-Kale-with-Lemon-Hemp-Seeds-and-Tahini-10-minutes-6-ingredients-BIG-flavor-plantbased-glutenfree-kale-minimalistbaker-recipe-8.jpg" data-pin-description="QUICK Sauteed Kale with Lemon, Hemp Seeds, and Tahini! <p>All that’s left to do is top with a squeeze of lemon, hemp seeds, and a drizzle of tahini! Swoon.</p>
<p>This kale is delicious on its own, paired with other cooked vegetables (like <a aria-label="roasted squash, sweet potatoes, and carrots (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-roast-vegetables/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">roasted squash, sweet potatoes, and carrots</a>), or in breakfast bowls with a <a aria-label="fried egg (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-cook-an-egg-runny-yolk/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">fried egg</a> (our favorite!).</p>

<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Heat a large pan (preferably <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QkLdIXnLFDHvTgGzGQcnkj8AAAFuTQKNKAEAAAFKAUP5xv8/http://www.amazon.com/dp/B00IWSQCI6/?creativeASIN=B00IWSQCI6&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=M.Gva19vh5BytB2WoGk5bQ&amp;slotNum=31&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">cast iron</a>) over medium-high heat. Once hot, add oil and swirl to coat. Wait 30 seconds for the oil to heat.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Add chopped kale and season with salt and curry powder (<em>optional</em>). Toss to coat. Then cover and cook for 1 minute.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Remove lid and stir/toss. Cover again and cook until kale is slightly wilted, vibrant green, and a little crispy in spots. The cooking process should only take about 2-3 minutes total.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Transfer from pan to serving plate and top with lemon juice, hemp seeds, and tahini. Enjoy. Store leftovers covered in the fridge and reheat on the stovetop over medium heat, or add to soups / stews. Best when fresh. Not freezer friendly.</div>
</li>
</ol>
