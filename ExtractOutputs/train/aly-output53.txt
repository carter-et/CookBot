<h2>How to Make Cauliflower Wings</h2>
<p> We find making cauliflower wings works best as a four-step process:</p>
<ol><li><strong>Egg wash</strong> – eggs are gently beaten with a fork to create an egg wash that is perfect for helping the batter stick to the cauliflower. <em>If egg-free or vegan, you can try subbing non-dairy milk as used in <a aria-label="this method (opens in a new tab)" href="https://minimalistbaker.com/cashew-crusted-cauliflower-steak/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">this method</a>.</em></li><li><strong>Batter</strong> – we used a cashew-arrowroot-salt-spice mix. You want the texture to be a semi-fine meal so that it sticks easily to the cauliflower.</li><li><strong>Bake</strong> – we like to bake at a high temp (425 F/218 C) for ~20 minutes to get the cauliflower wings nice and crispy. Placing on a cooling rack over a baking sheet helps the cauliflower crisp up without getting too brown on the bottom (though both methods work!).</li><li><strong>Glaze</strong> – once the cauliflower is baked, it is stirred into a warm glaze. Doing this after baking allows the cauliflower to stay crispy but also soak up delicious flavor!</li></ol>

<li class="wprm-recipe-ingredient">

</li>

<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-unit">Tbsp</span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-name">honey </span>

<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-amount">3</span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-unit">Tbsp</span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-name">hot sauce or buffalo sauce </span>
<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-notes">(we like <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Yellowbird-Habanero-Sauce-19-6-oz/dp/B00TBFMXGE/?tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Yellow Bird Habanero</a> for this recipe)</span>

<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-unit">pinch</span>

<span class="wprm-recipe-ingredient-notes">(<em><strong>optional</strong></em>)</span>
</li>

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (218 C) and line a large baking sheet with parchment paper. If you have a cooling rack that is oven safe, you can place that over the parchment paper; it will help the cauliflower crisp up even more. However, we tested it with and without and both work. The primary difference is when not using a cooling rack, the cauliflower undersides can get a little more brown. But the easy workaround is cooking the cauliflower in the upper third of the oven to prevent the bottoms from burning.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Add cauliflower florets to a large mixing bowl and season with a bit of salt and pepper (pepper optional). Toss to combine and set aside. (Reserve large mixing bowl for use later.)</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Crack eggs into a shallow bowl and whisk until combined. Set aside.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Next, prepare cashew coating by adding cashews, salt, cayenne pepper, garlic powder, paprika, and arrowroot starch (or cornstarch or potato starch) to a <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QukWf1ecNtVrdW1Td6Id-MoAAAFvkY1gaQEAAAFKAaVwtgI/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00LBZOYAK/?creativeASIN=B00LBZOYAK&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=y9UxVE3X4EkjRTU7IHTsuQ&amp;slotNum=9&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">food processor</a>. Mix until a semi-fine meal is achieved. A little texture is okay (see photo), but you want it pretty fine so it coats the cauliflower. Transfer coating to a shallow dish (shallow enough to dip the cauliflower in) and set aside.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Dip a few cauliflower florets at a time in the eggs, ensuring all sides are well coated. Then shake off excess. Transfer the cauliflower to the cashew coating and toss until thoroughly coated, using a spoon or your hands to add more coating to any bare spots.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Transfer cauliflower to your prepared baking sheet (with or without a cooling rack). Finally, drizzle or spritz with a little avocado oil to help them crisp up (optional).</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Bake for ~20 minutes or until the cauliflower appears golden brown. </p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>In the meantime, prepare glaze. To a large <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QnYOlA4a9R_y1exEVIKhxjMAAAFvkY6iJgEAAAFKAcfLfQQ/https://www.amazon.com/Dansk-833300-Kobenstyle-Saucepan-Medium/dp/B009SQIW2K/?creativeASIN=B009SQIW2K&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=zW17U8vLtxFyFC869EXxWw&amp;slotNum=27&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">saucepan</a>, add honey and hot sauce and a pinch of red pepper flake (optional). Heat over medium-low heat, stirring frequently, until it bubbles and appears combined. Set aside.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Transfer cooked cauliflower to the large mixing bowl from earlier and top with the glaze. Gently toss to combine, then serve immediately.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Best when fresh. Store leftovers covered in the refrigerator up to 3-4 days. Reheat in a 350 degree F (176 C) oven until hot for best results. Not freezer friendly.</div>
</li>
</ol>
</div>
