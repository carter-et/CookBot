You can use other type of dashi, but to achieve the “authentic” Japanese taste, kombu dashi is the key. I don’t recommend using chicken stock in this recipe, for example.</p> <p><strong>Sansai:</strong> The vegetable mix is the most difficult one to find for this recipe. But it is the main ingredient, and by substituting it, it basically becomes a different dish. If you’re curious to try, I recommend trying my other mixed rice recipes like <a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/takikomi-gohan/">Takikomi Gohan</a>, <a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/ginger-rice/">Ginger Rice</a>, <a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/sweet-onion-takikomi-gohan/">Sweet Onion Rice</a>, and <a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/gobo-and-miso-takikomi-gohan/">Gobo &amp; Miso Takikomi Gohan</a>.</p> <p><strong>Aburaage:</strong> You may notice that I include aburaage in most of my mixed rice recipe and there’s a good reason for that. The oil from the deep-fried tofu pouch gives amazing savory flavor without the addition of meat. When you’re making a dish with a simple layer, especially a vegetarian dish, toss in some aburaage can help to heighten the textures. I am also aware that this ingredient is much harder to find than <em>Inariage</em>, which is the seasoned tofu pouch often used for Inari Sushi. If you are going to use Inariage, remember it’s very sweet, so you have to adjust the seasonings.</p> <p><strong>Usukuchi Soy Sauce:</strong> This light-colored soy sauce is actually saltier than regular soy sauce.  In Kansai region of Japan (Osaka and Kyoto area), light-colored soy sauce is used to preserve the original color of the foods.  You can use regular soy sauce for this recipe, but remember it’s slightly less saltier than usukuchi soy sauce.</p> <p><strong>Sake &amp; Mirin:</strong> If you want to learn more about these two essential Japanese condiments, <a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/sake-mirin/">please click here</a>.</p> <p><strong>Kosher salt:</strong> I often receive questions why I add salt when soy sauce is already included. Well, both salt and soy sauce actually play different roles. Soy sauce is used to lend a savory profile, while salt is to enhance the overall flavor of the dish. When you are doing the final tasting of the dish, you can just adjust the amount of salt to boost the flavor without adding more soy sauce.</p> <p>With a hint of woodsy and earthy flavor, Sansai Gohan is a rustic dish that reminds me what a special time of year spring is. To enjoy, serve this mixed rice with a bowl of miso soup.</p> <p style="text-align:center;"><img alt="Rice with Mountain Vegetables (Sansai Gohan) served in a green ceramic bowl." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-68865 nitro-lazy nitro-lazy-empty" data-pin-description="Rice with Mountain Vegetables (Sansai Gohan 山菜ご飯) #JapaneseFood #Sansai #Gohan #Vegetarian #Vegan | Easy Japanese Recipes at JustOneCookbook.com" data-pin-title="Sansai Gohan (Rice with Mountain Vegetables) 山菜ご飯" height="400" id="MTU4MjM5MTcwMi4xMTg4" nitro-lazy-src="https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Sansai-Gohan-VI-600x400.jpg" nitro-lazy-srcset="https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Sansai-Gohan-VI.jpg 600w, https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Sansai-Gohan-VI-350x233.jpg 350w, https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Sansai-Gohan-VI-259x173.jpg 259w, https://d3eh3svpl1busq.cloudfront.net/KQYMGOLIdXGmoAcyJsPOrQDKktgCbwtG/assets/static/optimized/rev-c031098/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Sansai-Gohan-VI-300x200.jpg 300w" nitro-og-height="400" nitro-og-width="600" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" width="600"/></p> <p>Don’t want to miss a recipe?
