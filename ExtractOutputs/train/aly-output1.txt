What do I mean by “double”? Chocolate is used two times: cocoa powder and melted chocolate.</p> <p>I tried adjusting the ratio of the cocoa powder and melted chocolate to make the cake as light as possible, but the richer the chocolate cake is, the denser it gets. It loses the bouncy, airly, light “chiffon cake” texture. The cake resembles more like a pound cake, instead of a chiffon cake.</p> <p>Another issue I had with double chocolate was that the cake does not rise as tall as the chiffon cake with only cocoa powder. For example, I would have to use a 7″ (17 cm) chiffon cake pan for the ingredients for 8″ (20 cm) chiffon cake to get a decent “chiffon cake” height.</p> <p>In the end, I was not quite satisfied with the result (and can’t call that “chiffon cake”), so I increased the cocoa powder in the batter to get more chocolate flavor. My chocolate to cake flour ratio is <strong>1 to 2</strong> (40 g cocoa powder: 80 g cake flour). If you decrease the cocoa powder to 20 g (1 to 6 ratio), the cake will become taller, but less “chocolate-ty” taste.</p> <h2>How to Make Perfect Chiffon Cake?</h2> <p>Chiffon cakes are one of the easiest cakes you can make at home.
