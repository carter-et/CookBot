<p>If you’ve never made your own applesauce, you’re missing out! It’s one of those foods that tastes better homemade than store-bought. </p>
<p>Plus, it’s super simple to make! Our <strong>3-ingredient</strong> applesauce requires just <strong>25 minutes</strong> and <strong>1 pan</strong> to prepare. And it’s made without any added sugar. Just apples, lemon, and cinnamon. Let’s do this!</p>

<h2>How to Make Applesauce</h2>
<p>We told you it’s easy, but “<em>How eas</em>y?” you might ask!</p>
<ol><li>Peel and chop apples. You don’t even <em>need</em> to peel them if that’s more your style!</li><li>Add to a saucepan with cinnamon.</li><li>Cook 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.</li><li>Mash with a spoon or potato masher and add lemon juice.</li></ol>
<p>That’s it!</p>
<h2>Best Apples for Applesauce</h2>
<p>We prefer applesauce that doesn’t need any sugar added. Honeycrisps (or other sweet varieties) work well for creating a naturally sweet sauce.</p>
<p>For a tart applesauce, you can choose Granny Smith apples instead.</p>

<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>To a <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QogvOAdKEEbgsrF1J9WF9FcAAAFvkYrAKQEAAAFKAT0sVQU/https://www.amazon.com/Dansk-833300-Kobenstyle-Saucepan-Medium/dp/B009SQIW2K/?creativeASIN=B009SQIW2K&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=0JEqSJpS0h84uQpsrCevPw&amp;slotNum=27&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">medium saucepan</a> add apples and cinnamon. <strong>Cover</strong> and cook over medium heat until it simmers, then reduce heat to low / medium-low and continue cooking until the apples are tender and very slightly caramelized — about 15-20 minutes. Stir occasionally. </p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Once cooked, add lemon juice and stir. Then use the back of your spoon or a potato masher to mash into a loose sauce. You could also blend in a <a href="https://aax-us-east.amazon-adsystem.com/x/c/QuIW8Q-yYzFoghduLBCmsM4AAAFvkcNxnAEAAAFKAVD2MBU/https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00LBZOYAK/?creativeASIN=B00LBZOYAK&amp;linkCode=w61&amp;imprToken=aOROMdQwVEAI0-3HPnU4LA&amp;slotNum=9&amp;tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">food processor</a> for smoother texture (optional). </p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Enjoy fresh, or store cooled leftovers in the refrigerator up to 4-5 days, or in the freezer up to 1 month. We enjoy it cold, but it could also be heated in the microwave or on the stovetop over low heat until hot.</p></div>
</li>
</ol>
