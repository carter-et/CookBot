<p>So, now that we’ve <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/super-cleansing-slaw-rosemary-dressing/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">conquered our fear of fennel</a> (I mean, seriously, what do you do with this ridiculous-looking licorice bomb?), it’s time to move into uncharted, even more delicious territory: salads.</p>
<p>This salad is, in a word, irresistible. We dare you to take just one bite. Let us show you how this <strong>30-minute side or entrée</strong> is made!</p>

<p><span id="more-31323"></span></p>
<p>This start of this salad show is the fennel – <em>curry</em>–<em>roasted</em> fennel to be exact.</p>
<p>If you fear the pungent, licorice-y taste of fennel, roasting it in a little <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/diy-curry-powder/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">curry powder</a> and salt takes it to tasty town. (But let’s be real – any vegetable roasted in <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/diy-curry-powder/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">this curry powder</a> is out-of-this-world delicious. What do you expect? So, much, flavor.)</p>
<p><iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="380" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/yxJU-3CkLgA" width="680"></iframe></p>

<p>The next step? Dressing.</p>
<p>It was hard to decide on just one, but for simplicity’s sake, we suggest the Rosemary Tahini Dressing from this <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/super-cleansing-slaw-rosemary-dressing/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Amazing Super Cleansing Slaw</a>. The flavors just work.</p>
<p>But, if you’re looking to venture outside of your tahini comfort zone, try this <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/6-ingredient-vegan-tzatziki/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">6-ingredient Vegan Tzatziki</a>! We found that it melded really well with the complex flavor of the curry by adding a nice cooling, refreshing finish. You can’t go wrong either way.</p>
<p>Once your fennel is roasted, it’s assembly time. For even more protein, fiber, and crunch, we opted for a batch of our <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/actually-crispy-baked-chickpeas/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Actually Crispy Baked Chickpeas</a>. To save time, you could also just use cooked (unbaked) chickpeas or grab some already baked at the store.</p>

<p>We hope you LOVE this salad! It’s:</p>
<p>Flavorful<br/>
Hearty<br/>
Fresh<br/>
Crunchy<br/>
Easy to make<br/>
Versatile<br/>
&amp; Insanely delicious</p>
<p>This would make the perfect side to bring along to dinner parties or serve alongside mains like these <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/smoky-bbq-black-bean-burger/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Mushroom Black Bean Burgers</a> or <a href="https://minimalistbaker.com/sun-dried-tomato-chickpea-burgers/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Sun-Dried Tomato Chickpea Burgers</a>. Leaving the chickpeas off this salad makes it more of a <strong>side for two to four people</strong> (in our opinion). But <strong>to make it an entrée for two, add the chickpeas (or cooked quinoa!)</strong> for extra heartiness.</p>

<h2 class="wprm-recipe-name">Curry Roasted Fennel Salad with Rosemary Tahini Dressing</h2>
<div class="wprm-recipe-summary">

<div class="wprm-recipe-instructions-container">
<h3 class="wprm-recipe-header">Instructions</h3>
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-group">
<ol class="wprm-recipe-instructions">
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">If serving with crispy chickpeas, follow the link for instructions and prepare now. Otherwise, preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 C) and arrange fennel on a large baking sheet. Sprinkle with curry powder, coconut oil (or water), and sea salt and toss to combine. Spread in an even layer.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Roast fennel for about 20 minutes or until tender, golden brown, and slightly crispy on the edges. Set aside.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">In the meantime, prepare dressing by adding all ingredients (starting with the lesser measurement of water) to a small blender (such as a <a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B012T634SM/?tag=minimalistbaker-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Magic Bullet</a>) or a mixing bowl and blending or whisking to combine. Add more water as needed until creamy and pourable.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Taste and adjust flavor as needed, adding more garlic for zing, lemon for acidity, apple cider vinegar for tanginess, or rosemary for herbal flavor.</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">To serve, arrange greens of choice on a platter or serving bowl and top with red bell pepper, roasted fennel, chickpeas (optional), and any additional garnishes, such as fresh rosemary or lemon slices (optional).</div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text"><p>Serve with dressing on the side. This salad acts as a side without the chickpeas, and more of an entrée <i>with</i> the chickpeas.</p></div>
</li>
<li class="wprm-recipe-instruction">
<div class="wprm-recipe-instruction-text">Best when fresh. Store leftovers covered in the refrigerator up to 4 days. The dressing will keep in the refrigerator up to 6-7 days.</div>
</li>
</ol>
